What to Expect on Your First Visit

First visits can feel a bit daunting — here's what to expect at St Mary's, so you can arrive feeling relaxed and ready.

1. You'll Be Greeted

A friendly steward will welcome you at the door, hand you an order of service, and answer any questions. You won't be left to fend for yourself!

2. The Service

Sunday services last about an hour and include hymns, prayers, Bible readings, and a short sermon. Children are welcome throughout, and Junior Church begins at the same time for ages 4–11.

3. Tea & Coffee

After the service, everyone gathers for tea, coffee, and biscuits. This is the real heart of St Mary's — a relaxed time to chat, make friends, and feel at home.

What to Wear

Come as you are — there's no dress code at St Mary's. Some people dress smartly, others come in jeans and trainers. What matters is that you're here.

No Pressure

You won't be asked to stand up, introduce yourself, or put money in a collection. You're simply invited to experience worship and community at your own pace.

Bringing Children

Families with young children are very welcome! Junior Church runs simultaneously with the service, and children are welcome to sit with families throughout. Babies and toddlers are always welcome — don't worry about noise!

Getting Here

  • By Car: Handforth is located just off the A34, between Wilmslow and Cheadle Hulme. Follow signs for Handforth village.
  • By Train: Handforth railway station is a short walk from the church, served by trains from Manchester Piccadilly and Crewe.
  • By Bus: Several bus routes serve Handforth village centre. Check TfGM for current timetables.
  • Parking: On-street parking is available in the surrounding streets. Please be considerate of neighbours.

🚗 Accessibility Note

Our main entrance is step-free. Blue Badge parking is available nearby. If you need any assistance, please let us know in advance and we'll be happy to help.
